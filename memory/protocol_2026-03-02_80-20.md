## Weekly Philosophy
- **80% Easy**: Conversational pace, <75% max HR, focus on technique and aerobic base
- **20% Hard**: High-intensity intervals, tempo work, race-pace efforts
- **NO grey zone**: All workouts are clearly easy or clearly hard
- **Goal**: Build aerobic capacity while maintaining strength and introducing race-pace efforts

## Weekly Schedule

| Day | Type | Intensity | Workout | Duration |
|-----|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Monday** | Swim | Easy (80%) | 1000m easy freestyle: 200m warm-up + 6x100m easy (rest 20s between) + 200m cool-down. Focus on breathing rhythm and form. | 30 min |
| **Tuesday** | Bike + Strength | Hard (20%) | **Bike**: 10min warm-up + 4x4min hard intervals (threshold, 85-90% HR) at 90-95 RPM + 10min cool-down. **Then**: Strength A (see below) | 45 min bike + 35 min strength |
| **Wednesday** | Run | Easy (80%) | 4 miles easy run. Conversational pace, HR <150. Focus on cadence and form. | 40 min |
| **Thursday** | Run + Strength | Hard (20%) | **Run**: 10min easy warm-up + 6x800m hard (90%+ max HR, ~4:45 pace) with 3min easy jog recovery + 10min easy cool-down. **Then**: Strength B (see below) | 50 min run + 35 min strength |
| **Friday** | Swim | Easy (80%) | 800m easy swim: 200m warm-up + 4x100m freestyle + 4x50m kick + 200m cool-down. Mix of drills and steady pace. | 25 min |
| **Saturday** | Brick (Long) | Hard (20%) | **Bike**: 60min ride (20min easy warm-up + 30min at 80-85 RPM steady pace + 10min cool-down). **Run**: Immediately transition to 20min easy run off-bike to practice transitions. | 80 min total |
| **Sunday** | Active Recovery | Easy (80%) | 30min easy walk or very easy swim. Focus on moving, not training. Stretching and foam rolling. | 30 min |

## Strength A (Tuesday, after bike)

**Circuit format** (minimize rest between exercises):

| Exercise | Weight | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Incline Bench Press | 120 lbs | 3 x 8-10 | 1 RIR — stop when you could do 1 more rep |
| Cable Seated Row | 145 lbs | 3 x 11-13 | 1 RIR |
| Assisted Chin-ups | 70 lbs assist | 3 x 4-5 | 1 RIR |
| Dumbbell Lying Tricep Extension | 20 lbs | 3 x 10-12 | 1 RIR |

**Rest**: 60-90 seconds between exercises. Total time: ~35 min.

**Rationale**: Lighter session on Tuesday (hard bike day). Focus on maintaining strength, not building. Compound pulls + upper body only.

## Strength B (Thursday, after run)

**Full Body** (alternating upper/lower):

| Exercise | Weight | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Dumbbell Bench Press | 65 lbs | 3 x 10-12 | 1 RIR |
| Dumbbell Rows | 75 lbs | 3 x 12-14 | 1 RIR |
| Machine Leg Press | 330 lbs | 3 x 10-12 | 1 RIR |
| Lying Hamstring Curls | 130 lbs | 3 x 10-12 | 1 RIR |

**Rest**: 90-120 seconds between exercises. Total time: ~35 min.

**Rationale**: Thursday is hard run day, so strength is balanced (full body but controlled intensity).

| Zone | HR Range | RPE | Pace/Effort | % of Workouts |
|------|----------|-----|------------|-----------------|
| **Easy (Z1-Z2)** | <75% max | 4-5/10 | Conversational | 80% |
| **Hard (Z4-Z5)** | 85-95% max | 8-10/10 | Race pace to threshold | 20% |
| **Transition (20-30s)** | Cooldown | 2-3/10 | Recovery | Between efforts |

## Weekly Targets

**Swim Volume**: 2,800m (3 sessions)
- Easy pace focus, build to 3-4km/week by W12

**Bike Volume**: 105 minutes (2 sessions)
- Threshold intervals + one long easy session
- Build to 5+ hours by W14

**Run Volume**: 14 miles (3 sessions)
- Threshold intervals + long brick
- Build to 20+ km by W14

**Strength**: 2x/week (maintenance)
- Upper body focus on Tuesday (lighter after hard bike)
- Full body on Thursday (balanced after hard run)
- Goal: Preserve strength, 1 RIR discipline

## Progression Plan (Next 4 Weeks)

## How to Log

**Every session, log:**
1. Swim: distance (meters), average pace, notes on form
2. Bike: duration (min), avg power (if available), HR zones
3. Run: distance (miles), average pace, HR zones
4. Strength: weight x reps for each exercise

**Weekly check-in:**
- Total volume (swim + bike run hours)
- Hard workout compliance (did you hit the targets?)
- Sleep quality + mood (recovery indicator)
- Any pain/aches?

## Notes

- **Strength is secondary** during triathlon build. Focus on hard cardio first.
- **Recovery is training**: Easy days are NOT rest days — they're low-intensity volume.
- **Heart rate matters**: Invest in a decent HR monitor (Garmin watch) to stay in zones.
- **Transitions matter**: Saturday brick teaches your legs to run hard off the bike.
- **Strength maintenance**: 1 RIR discipline prevents overtraining while preserving muscle.

## 16-Week Path to Half Ironman (Oct 2026)

- **W10-W13** (Mar-Apr): Base Building — establish aerobic foundation, introduce hard efforts
- **W14-W20** (May-Jun): Build Phase — increase volume, add race-pace work
- **W21-W23** (Jul-Aug): Peak Phase — long course simulation, taper begins
- **W24** (Sep): Taper — reduce volume 40-50%, maintain intensity
- **Race Week** (Oct): Final taper, race readiness
